MS Plates & Sheets - Types & Configurations

Hot Rolled (HR) Plates & Sheets

Manufacturing Process: Hot rolling above recrystallization temperature (typically 1200¬∞C) produces plates and sheets with characteristic black mill scale surface finish. The process involves heating steel billets to austenitic phase and passing through progressive rolling mill stands, reducing thickness by 40-60% per pass until final gauge is achieved.

Dimensional Range: Thickness from 2mm to 100mm in plate category, 0.5mm to 6mm for sheet category. Standard widths 1000mm, 1250mm, 1500mm, 2000mm, 2500mm accommodating most fabrication requirements. Lengths available in 2000mm, 2400mm, 3000mm, 6000mm, or custom cut-to-length service. Tolerance per IS 1852 Grade A (¬±0.3mm for <6mm thickness).

Cold Rolled (CR) Sheets

Production Method: Cold reduction process performed at room temperature on pickled hot-rolled coils, producing superior surface finish, tighter dimensional tolerances, and improved mechanical properties. Multiple rolling passes achieve 50-90% thickness reduction with intermediate annealing to restore ductility and prevent work hardening.

Surface Quality: Bright, smooth, scale-free finish ideal for painting, powder coating, and visible applications. Surface roughness Ra 0.5-1.5 microns compared to 3-6 microns for hot rolled. Available in commercial quality (CQ), drawing quality (DQ), deep drawing quality (DDQ), and extra deep drawing quality (EDDQ) grades. Thickness range 0.3mm to 3.2mm with tolerance ¬±0.05mm.

Plate Thickness Categories & Applications

Thin Plates (2-6mm): General fabrication work, automotive body panels, electrical enclosures, HVAC ductwork, and light structural components. Easy to shear, punch, and form with standard tooling. Suitable for TIG/MIG welding without preheating.

Medium Plates (8-25mm): Machinery bases, pressure vessel shells, structural reinforcement, crane girders, and moderate-load applications. Requires plasma cutting or oxy-fuel for thick sections. Preheat 100-150¬∞C recommended for welding to prevent cracking.

Heavy Plates (30-100mm): Bridge decking, shipbuilding hull plates, offshore platform structures, heavy press frames, and critical load-bearing members. Ultrasonic testing (UT) for internal defect detection. Controlled rolling and normalizing heat treatment for improved toughness and through-thickness properties.

Coil vs. Cut Sheet Supply

Coil Form: Master coils weighing 5-25 tons with inside diameter 508mm or 610mm, suitable for high-volume manufacturing with automated slitting, blanking, and roll-forming lines. Eliminates butt-welding joints in continuous production. Length up to 2000 meters depending on thickness and width.

Cut Sheets: Sheared or slit to specified dimensions from master coils, ready for immediate fabrication without further processing. Standard sizes 4'x8' (1220x2440mm), 5'x10' (1500x3000mm), or custom blanks optimized to minimize scrap. Edge quality: mill edge (as-slit), sheared edge, or precision-slit edge (<0.5mm burr height).

Material Grades & Specifications

IS 2062 - Indian Standard Structural Steel

Grade Variants: E250 (250 MPa yield strength) for general structural and fabrication work, E300 (300 MPa) for moderate-stress applications, E350 (350 MPa) and E410 (410 MPa) for high-strength structural components. A, B, C quality designations indicate impact toughness at 0¬∞C, -20¬∞C, -40¬∞C respectively.

Chemical Composition: Carbon content 0.20-0.25% maximum for weldability, Manganese 1.50% max for strength, Sulphur and Phosphorus <0.045% each to prevent hot shortness and cold brittleness. Silicon 0.40% max as deoxidizer. Controlled rolling practice for fine grain structure and improved notch toughness.

ASTM A36 - Carbon Structural Steel

Mechanical Properties: Minimum yield strength 250 MPa (36 ksi), tensile strength 400-550 MPa (58-80 ksi), elongation 20% minimum in 200mm gauge length. Excellent ductility and formability for bending, stamping, and deep drawing operations without cracking.

Weldability: Low carbon equivalent (CE) of 0.40-0.45% enables welding without preheat for thickness up to 25mm using SMAW, GMAW, SAW, and FCAW processes. Hydrogen-controlled electrodes (H5 or lower) prevent cold cracking in restrained joints. Post-weld heat treatment (PWHT) optional for stress relief.

Additional Material Standards

ASTM A283: Low and intermediate tensile strength carbon steel plates for general structural and pressure vessel applications. Grades A, B, C, D with tensile strength 310-450 MPa. Primarily used in welded, riveted, or bolted construction of bridges, buildings, and storage tanks.

ASTM A572: High-strength low-alloy (HSLA) columbium-vanadium structural steel with yield strengths 290-450 MPa (Grades 42, 50, 55, 60, 65). Micro-alloyed for grain refinement, providing superior strength-to-weight ratio. Used in weight-critical structures like transmission towers, cranes, and mobile equipment.

EN 10025: European standard covering non-alloy structural steels S235 (235 MPa yield), S275, S355, S460. Normalized or thermomechanical rolled (M, N designations) for improved toughness. Impact testing at specified temperatures ensures brittle fracture resistance in cold climates.

Surface Treatments & Finishing Options

Pickling & Oiling (P&O)

Process: Hot rolled coils pass through hydrochloric or sulphuric acid bath (5-15% concentration at 60-80¬∞C) removing mill scale and rust, followed by water rinse, drying, and protective oil coating. Results in clean, grey metallic surface free from scale but retaining hot rolled texture and dimensional characteristics.

Benefits: Improved paint adhesion versus black mill scale, reduced spatter in welding, easier quality inspection of surface defects, and temporary corrosion protection during storage/transport (3-6 months indoors). Oil film thickness 1-2 g/m¬≤ using mineral oil or water-soluble rust preventive compounds. Ideal for subsequent cold rolling, galvanizing, or painting operations.

Shot Blasting & Abrasive Cleaning

Mechanical Descaling: High-velocity steel shot or grit (0.3-2.0mm diameter) propelled by centrifugal wheel or compressed air impacts surface, removing mill scale, rust, and contaminants. Cleanliness grades per ISO 8501-1: Sa 2 (thorough blast), Sa 2¬Ω (very thorough blast), Sa 3 (blast to white metal). Surface profile 25-75 microns Rz for optimal coating adhesion.

Applications: Pre-treatment for heavy-duty protective coatings (epoxy, polyurethane, inorganic zinc) in corrosive environments. Removes welding slag, heat treat scale, and surface defects. Creates anchor pattern for paint mechanical bonding, increasing coating life 3-5x versus unprepared surfaces. Shot peening variant induces compressive residual stresses, improving fatigue strength 20-30%.

Additional Surface Options

Mill Finish: As-rolled surface with black mill scale (iron oxide) formed during hot rolling. Rust-like appearance, uneven texture, suitable only for non-cosmetic applications where subsequent cleaning/coating will be performed. Lowest cost option, typically 10-15% cheaper than pickled material.

Bright Annealed: Cold rolled sheets annealed in controlled atmosphere (hydrogen or dissociated ammonia) preventing oxidation, resulting in bright, reflective, oxide-free surface. Used for decorative applications, electronic enclosures, and components requiring subsequent plating without additional surface preparation. Premium pricing, 25-40% above standard cold rolled.

Key Features & Performance Benefits

Excellent Formability & Ductility

Low carbon content (0.15-0.25%) provides superior ductility with 20-30% elongation, enabling complex forming operations including deep drawing, bending (minimum radius 1.5t for CR, 2t for HR), stamping, and roll forming without cracking. Cold rolled grades DDQ/EDDQ achieve 38-42% elongation for automotive body panels and appliance shells. Uniform mechanical properties ensure consistent forming behavior.

Superior Weldability

Low carbon equivalent (0.40-0.45%) enables welding without preheat up to 25mm thickness using all common processes: SMAW (stick), GMAW (MIG), GTAW (TIG), SAW (submerged arc), FCAW (flux-cored). Minimal weld defects (porosity, cracking, spatter) with proper technique. No special electrodes required for most applications. Excellent fusion and penetration characteristics reduce rework and inspection costs.

Cost-Effective Solution

Mild steel plates and sheets offer lowest cost per kilogram among structural materials, typically 30-50% less than alloy steels, 60-80% less than stainless steel, and comparable to aluminum on volume basis while providing 2.5x higher strength. High material availability and standardization reduce lead times. Extensive fabrication infrastructure and skilled workforce minimize processing costs. Recyclable with minimal value loss.

Versatile Applications

Suitable for 80% of structural steel applications from simple brackets to complex pressure vessels. Compatible with all common joining methods: welding, bolting, riveting, adhesive bonding. Accepts full range of surface treatments: painting, powder coating, galvanizing, plating. Machines readily with HSS and carbide tooling. Available in wide range of thicknesses, widths, and lengths minimizing material waste through efficient nesting.

Galvanizing Process & Types

Hot-Dip Galvanizing (HDG) Process

Manufacturing Method: Cold rolled steel strip passes through molten zinc bath at 450-460¬∞C, forming metallurgically bonded zinc-iron alloy layers beneath pure zinc outer layer. Total coating weight 80-600 g/m¬≤ (40-300 g/m¬≤ per side). Line speed 60-200 m/min through continuous galvanizing line (CGL) with air knife thickness control achieving ¬±10% coating uniformity.

Coating Structure: Multi-layer coating consists of eta layer (pure zinc, 90% of thickness), zeta layer (FeZn13), delta layer (FeZn7), and gamma layer (Fe3Zn10) bonded to substrate. Zinc-iron intermetallic layers provide superior adhesion preventing coating delamination during forming. Spangle pattern (crystalline zinc grain structure) ranges from minimized spangle for painting to regular spangle for roofing aesthetics.

Electrogalvanized (EG) Steel

Electroplating Process: Zinc deposited via electrochemical reduction in zinc sulfate or zinc chloride electrolyte bath. Current density 20-100 A/dm¬≤ produces controlled coating thickness 2.5-20 microns per side with exceptional uniformity (¬±2 microns). Brightness and smoothness superior to hot-dip, ideal for exposed painted surfaces in automotive and appliances.

Characteristics: Thin, ductile coating permits tighter bend radii (0.5t vs 2t for HDG) and severe forming without coating fracture. Excellent paint adhesion and surface smoothness eliminate orange peel. Lower coating weight reduces material cost but provides 5-10 year corrosion protection versus 15-25 years for HDG in similar environments. Common in automotive body panels, furniture, and consumer electronics.

Coating Weight Classifications

Light Coating (80-120 GSM): Total coating weight 80-120 g/m¬≤ (40-60 g/m¬≤ per side, 5-8 micron thickness) for indoor or sheltered applications. Suitable for painted roofing, wall cladding, and HVAC ductwork with 7-12 year bare corrosion life. Most economical option for non-critical exterior use.

Standard Coating (120-180 GSM): 120-180 g/m¬≤ total weight (60-90 g/m¬≤ per side, 8-13 microns) provides balanced corrosion protection and formability for general construction, purlins, girts, and structural framing. Expected 12-18 year service life in typical rural/suburban atmospheric exposure per ISO 9223 C2/C3 categories.

Heavy Coating (180-275 GSM): 180-275 g/m¬≤ total weight (90-137.5 g/m¬≤ per side, 13-19 microns) for severe corrosive environments including coastal areas (within 1km of ocean), industrial zones with SO2/chloride exposure, and high-humidity tropical climates. 20-30 year durability with proper maintenance. Thicker coating reduces formability, minimum bend radius 3-4t.

Zinc-Aluminum Coatings (Galvalume/Zincalume)

Composition: 55% aluminum, 43.4% zinc, 1.6% silicon alloy coating (AZ150 typical) provides superior corrosion resistance versus pure zinc through aluminum's passive oxide protection. Coating weight 150 g/m¬≤ yields 2-4x longer life than equivalent zinc coating in most environments. Heat reflectivity 65-70% reduces roof temperatures 10-15¬∞C.

Performance: Excellent edge corrosion resistance (aluminum barrier effect), high-temperature oxidation resistance to 315¬∞C (roofing fire rating), and superior paintability with minimal chromate passivation. Ideal for unpainted roofing, architectural panels, and agricultural buildings. Cut edge protection inferior to zinc (no sacrificial protection), requiring edge sealing in critical applications. Service life 25-40 years in C2/C3 environments.

Material Standards & Grade Specifications

IS 277 - Indian Standard for Galvanized Sheets

Grade Designations: Soft (annealed) for maximum ductility and deep drawing, Structural for general forming and construction (230-280 MPa yield), High Strength (280-360 MPa) for load-bearing applications. Surface quality ordinary (minor defects acceptable), commercial (smooth appearance), and drawing quality (defect-free for exposed surfaces).

Coating Classifications: E (80 GSM minimum), D (120 GSM), C (180 GSM), B (240 GSM), A (275 GSM) with specified minimum coating weights ensuring 15-year guaranteed life in moderately corrosive (C3) environments. Coating adherence tested per bend test (180¬∞ bend over 1t mandrel without flaking) and impact test. Spangle finish: regular spangle, minimized spangle, or spangle-free.

ASTM A653/A653M - Galvanized Steel Sheets

Structural Grades: SS Grade 33 (230 MPa yield), SS Grade 40 (280 MPa), SS Grade 50 (345 MPa), SS Grade 80 (550 MPa) for progressively higher strength applications. HSLAS (High Strength Low Alloy Steel) grades provide enhanced strength-to-weight ratio through micro-alloying. Elongation 10-22% depending on grade and thickness, enabling moderate forming operations.

Coating Designations: G30 (90 g/m¬≤), G60 (180 g/m¬≤), G90 (275 g/m¬≤), G140 (425 g/m¬≤), G210 (640 g/m¬≤) indicate total coating weight both sides. A coating designation (A40, A60) specifies differential coating (heavier on one side) for applications with asymmetric corrosion exposure. Zinc-5% aluminum (ZA) and zinc-aluminum-magnesium (ZM) alloy coatings available for enhanced performance.

European & International Standards

EN 10346: European standard for continuously hot-dip coated steel flat products. Grade S220GD, S250GD, S280GD, S320GD, S350GD (yield strength in MPa). Surface treatment A (minimized spangle), B (skin-passed), N (normal spangle). Coating mass Z100 to Z600 (100-600 g/m¬≤ total). +Z (zinc), +ZM (zinc-magnesium), +AZ (zinc-aluminum) coating designations.

JIS G3302: Japanese Industrial Standard for hot-dip zinc-coated steel sheets. SGH340 (340 MPa tensile), SGH400, SGH440, SGH490, SGH540 for structural applications. SGCC (commercial), SGCD (drawing quality) for forming grades. Z12, Z18, Z22, Z27 coating thickness codes (12-27 microns). Chromate treatment types 00, 01, 1C for corrosion resistance and paintability.

Surface Treatments & Finishing

Chromate Passivation & Post-Treatment

Passivation Process: Chemical conversion coating applied immediately after galvanizing using chromic acid or chromate salts, forming thin (0.01-0.1 micron) protective layer. Hexavalent chromate (traditional) provides excellent corrosion inhibition and paint adhesion. Trivalent chromate (RoHS compliant) offers lower toxicity with slightly reduced performance. Passivation prevents white rust (zinc corrosion products) during storage and transit.

Advanced Treatments: Zinc phosphate conversion coating (1-5 microns) provides superior paint adhesion and corrosion protection beneath organic coatings. Thin-film organic coatings (0.5-2 microns epoxy, acrylic) further enhance pre-painted durability. Fingerprint-resistant coatings maintain aesthetic appearance on exposed surfaces. Anti-fingerprint treatments using silane or acrylic-based films prevent handling marks in appliance and automotive applications.

Pre-Painted & Color-Coated GI (PPGI)

Coating Systems: Factory-applied organic coatings on galvanized substrate through continuous coil coating line. Primer (5-8 microns) + topcoat (15-25 microns) on exposed side, backer coat (5-10 microns) on reverse. Total dry film thickness 20-35 microns. Polyester (PE) for general building, silicon-modified polyester (SMP) for improved color retention, polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) for 20+ year warranty in harsh environments.

Performance Benefits: Factory finish quality superior to field painting with uniform thickness, complete cure, and defect-free application. Wide color range (RAL, Pantone) with gloss levels 10-80 GU. UV resistance, chemical resistance, and scratch resistance per ASTM D3363 (pencil hardness H-3H). Eliminates on-site painting costs, reduces construction time, and provides predictable long-term appearance. Used in roofing, wall panels, garage doors, appliances.

Embossing & Texturing

Embossed Patterns: Decorative or functional surface textures rolled into galvanized sheet including stucco, pebble, leather grain, linen, and tile patterns. Pattern depth 0.05-0.3mm increases surface area 15-30%, improving paint adhesion and hiding minor surface imperfections. Anti-slip patterns for flooring, walkways, and work platforms provide coefficient of friction >0.5 wet/dry.

Corrugated Profiles: Sinusoidal, trapezoidal, or box-rib profiles roll-formed for roofing and wall cladding. Profile depth 10-40mm, pitch 50-200mm provides structural rigidity, spanning 1-4 meters between supports depending on gauge and profile geometry. Capillary channels and anti-capillary ribs prevent water ingress at laps. Pre-cut lengths 3-12 meters or custom cut-to-length eliminate field cutting and edge corrosion concerns.

Key Features & Performance Benefits

Superior Corrosion Protection

Zinc coating provides triple-barrier protection: physical barrier preventing moisture/oxygen contact, galvanic (sacrificial) protection where zinc corrodes preferentially protecting steel even at cut edges and scratches, and stable zinc carbonate patina formation in atmospheric exposure. HDG coating life 15-30 years in C2/C3 environments without maintenance. Coastal and industrial environments achieve 10-20 years with heavier coatings (240-275 GSM).

Cost-Effective Lifecycle Value

Initial material cost 15-25% premium over black (uncoated) steel, but eliminates painting costs (material + labor + downtime), extends maintenance intervals 3-5x, and provides 20-40 year service life versus 5-10 years for painted mild steel. Total cost of ownership 40-60% lower than paint systems over 25-year building life. Salvage value retained due to 100% recyclability of zinc and steel with no separation required.

Excellent Formability

Annealed substrate (soft temper) enables cold forming operations including roll forming (roof panels), brake bending (flashings, trims), deep drawing (air ducts), and stamping (electrical boxes) without coating fracture or delamination. Minimum bend radius 1.5-2t for standard coating, 2-3t for heavy coating. Zinc's low melting point (420¬∞C) permits welding, though coating vaporization requires adequate ventilation and weld zone touch-up with zinc-rich paint.

Sustainable & Eco-Friendly

Zinc coating extends steel life 3-10x, reducing resource extraction and embodied energy amortized over lifecycle. 100% recyclable indefinitely without quality loss‚Äîrecycled galvanized steel yields zinc for re-galvanizing and steel for new products. Galvanizing process 90-95% material efficient with minimal waste. Zinc runoff non-toxic to aquatic life at concentrations present in rainwater. Lower lifecycle CO2 emissions versus alternatives requiring frequent repainting/replacement.

Stainless Steel Grades & Metallurgy

SS 304 (18-8) - Austenitic Chromium-Nickel

Chemical Composition: 18-20% Chromium, 8-10.5% Nickel, ‚â§0.08% Carbon, ‚â§2% Manganese, ‚â§1% Silicon, balance Iron. Chromium forms stable, invisible passive chromium oxide (Cr‚ÇÇO‚ÇÉ) layer 1-3 nanometers thick, self-healing when damaged in oxidizing environments. Nickel stabilizes austenitic (FCC) crystal structure providing non-magnetic properties and excellent ductility at cryogenic to elevated temperatures.

Properties: Tensile strength 515-720 MPa, yield strength 205-310 MPa (annealed condition), elongation 40-50%, hardness 70-90 HRB. Excellent formability with deep drawing capability. Corrosion resistance in fresh water, atmospheric, mild acidic/alkaline environments. Service temperature -196¬∞C to +800¬∞C (brief exposure). Non-magnetic in annealed state, slightly magnetic after cold work. Most widely used stainless steel grade (50% of production).

SS 316 / 316L - Molybdenum-Enhanced Marine Grade

Enhanced Composition: 16-18% Chromium, 10-14% Nickel, 2-3% Molybdenum, ‚â§0.08% Carbon (316) or ‚â§0.03% Carbon (316L low-carbon variant). Molybdenum significantly improves resistance to chloride-induced pitting and crevice corrosion, critical for marine, coastal, and saline environments. Low-carbon 316L variant eliminates sensitization (grain boundary chromium carbide precipitation) during welding, maintaining corrosion resistance in heat-affected zones.

Superior Performance: Pitting resistance equivalent number (PREN = %Cr + 3.3√ó%Mo + 16√ó%N) of 24-26 versus 18-20 for SS304, quantifying chloride resistance. Withstands seawater, brine, phosphoric acid, acetic acid, and pharmaceutical/food processing chemicals. Essential for marine hardware, chemical processing equipment, pulp/paper mills, surgical implants, and pharmaceutical manufacturing. Cost premium 50-80% over SS304 justified in corrosive applications where SS304 would fail prematurely.

Specialty Stainless Steel Grades

SS 321 (Titanium-Stabilized): SS304 base with titanium addition (5√ó%C minimum) preventing sensitization during prolonged high-temperature exposure (425-815¬∞C). Ideal for aircraft exhaust manifolds, expansion joints, heat exchangers, and high-temperature piping where welding without solution annealing is required. Maintains corrosion resistance after heating without requiring PWHT.

SS 430 (Ferritic): 16-18% Chromium, low nickel (<0.75%), ferritic (BCC) crystal structure. Magnetic, lower cost (40% less than SS304), good oxidation resistance but inferior corrosion resistance versus austenitic grades. Moderate formability, suitable for automotive trim, appliance panels, and indoor architectural applications. Cannot be hardened by heat treatment. Service temperature -50¬∞C to +815¬∞C.

Duplex 2205 (Ferritic-Austenitic): 22% Cr, 5% Ni, 3% Mo, 50/50 ferrite/austenite microstructure yielding twice the strength (450-550 MPa yield) of SS304 with superior pitting/stress corrosion cracking resistance (PREN 34-36). Enables weight reduction in pressure vessels, heat exchangers, offshore platforms. Excellent weldability without preheating or PWHT. Limited formability requires hot forming above 950¬∞C for complex shapes.

Surface Finishes & Treatments

Standard Mill Finishes (ASTM A480)

No. 1 Finish (Hot Rolled, Annealed, Pickled): Dull, rough surface with visible heat scale after hot rolling, followed by annealing and acid pickling (mixed nitric-hydrofluoric acid) to remove scale. Surface roughness Ra 1.5-3.0 microns. Used for industrial applications where appearance is secondary: tanks, piping, structural components requiring subsequent surface treatment or insulation covering.

No. 2B Finish (Cold Rolled, Bright Annealed): Smooth, moderately reflective finish produced by cold rolling and bright annealing in controlled atmosphere (hydrogen or dissociated ammonia) preventing oxidation. Surface roughness Ra 0.5-1.0 microns. Most common general-purpose finish balancing cost and appearance. Suitable for most architectural, food processing, and equipment applications. Accepts polishing, painting, or use as-is.

No. 2D Finish (Cold Rolled, Dull): Similar to 2B but with dull, non-reflective surface from final cold rolling pass with dull rollers. Surface roughness Ra 0.8-1.5 microns. Preferred where low glare or subsequent drawing/forming is required. Better paint adhesion than brighter finishes. Common in automotive trim, appliance components, and stamped parts receiving powder coat or paint.

Mechanical Polished Finishes

No. 3 Finish (Ground): Coarse abrasive belt grinding (100-120 grit) producing uniform, unidirectional scratch pattern. Surface roughness Ra 0.5-0.8 microns. Used in food processing equipment, commercial kitchen surfaces, and architectural panels where moderate appearance and cleanability are required. Hides fingerprints better than brighter finishes. Cost-effective upgrade from 2B for visible surfaces.

No. 4 Finish (Brushed/Satin): Fine abrasive belt polishing (150-180 grit) creating directional satin appearance with fine, parallel grain lines. Surface roughness Ra 0.4-0.6 microns. Most popular architectural finish for column covers, elevator doors, kitchen equipment, handrails, and decorative panels. Excellent cleanability, hides minor scratches, maintains appearance under normal wear. Standard for commercial food service equipment per NSF requirements.

No. 6 Finish (Dull Satin): Tampico brushing or fine abrasive polishing (220-320 grit) followed by soft buffing, producing lower sheen than No. 4. Surface roughness Ra 0.3-0.5 microns. Less reflective, minimizes glare in lighting fixtures, architectural applications. Easier maintenance versus mirror finishes as minor scratches blend into existing texture. Common in healthcare and laboratory environments.

High-Gloss & Mirror Finishes

No. 7 Finish (High Luster): Progressively finer grit grinding (400-600 grit) followed by buffing with medium-cut compounds, producing high reflectivity but with slight directional lines visible. Surface roughness Ra 0.15-0.25 microns. Reflectivity 75-85%. Used in automotive trim, consumer products, and architectural accents where near-mirror appearance is desired at lower cost than true mirror finish. Shows fingerprints readily, requiring regular maintenance.

No. 8 Finish (Mirror/Reflective): Finest mechanical polishing using successively finer abrasives (800-2000 grit) and buffing compounds, producing defect-free mirror reflection. Surface roughness Ra 0.05-0.15 microns, reflectivity >90%. Most expensive finish requiring skilled labor and multiple polishing stages. Applications include decorative panels, luxury architectural features, food processing, pharmaceutical equipment where both aesthetics and hygiene are critical. Requires protective PVC film during fabrication/installation.

BA Finish (Bright Annealed): Cold rolled and bright annealed in protective atmosphere without subsequent mechanical finishing, producing smooth, reflective surface in coil form. Surface roughness Ra 0.2-0.4 microns, uniform appearance across entire coil. Lower cost than mechanical polishing, ideal for mass production of appliance liners, cookware, and light reflectors. Surface slightly less reflective than No. 8 but more cost-effective for large volumes.

Specialty Surface Treatments

Embossed/Patterned: Decorative patterns rolled into sheet surface: linen, leather grain, stucco, checkered, diamond plate. Pattern depth 0.05-0.3mm provides visual interest and slip resistance while hiding minor defects. Common in elevator interiors, wall panels, and anti-slip flooring. Easier cleaning as dirt accumulates in recesses rather than visible flat surface. Available in various finishes (2B, No. 4) applied before or after embossing.

PVD Coating (Physical Vapor Deposition): Vacuum deposition of titanium nitride (TiN), titanium carbide (TiC), or chromium nitride (CrN) creating gold, bronze, black, or blue metallic colors with exceptional durability. Coating thickness 0.5-3 microns, Vickers hardness 1500-2500 HV versus 200 HV for base SS. Scratch and abrasion resistance 5-10x greater than painted finishes. Architectural applications: elevator panels, column covers, signage. No color fade, maintains appearance 20+ years indoors.

Electropolishing: Reverse electroplating process removing surface metal (5-30 microns) via anodic dissolution in phosphoric-sulfuric acid electrolyte, producing ultra-smooth, passive surface. Surface roughness improved 30-50%, micro-peaks removed enhancing corrosion resistance 30% and reducing bacterial adhesion 99%. Critical for pharmaceutical vessels, food processing, surgical instruments per FDA/GMP requirements. Deburrs edges, brightens welds, enhances cleanability for sterile environments.

Mechanical Properties & Fabrication Characteristics

Strength & Formability

Annealed Condition: Solution annealed at 1040-1120¬∞C and rapidly cooled dissolves carbides, producing soft, ductile, non-magnetic structure. SS304: 205 MPa yield, 515 MPa tensile, 40% elongation. SS316: 220 MPa yield, 520 MPa tensile, 40% elongation. Suitable for deep drawing, severe bending (1t radius), hydroforming, and explosive forming. Hardness 70-90 HRB permits efficient machining with carbide tooling.

Work Hardening: Austenitic stainless exhibits rapid strain hardening during cold work, strength increasing 2-3x while ductility decreases. Quarter-hard (1/4H) temper: 380 MPa yield, half-hard (1/2H): 520 MPa yield, full-hard (H): 690 MPa yield. Work-hardened material becomes slightly magnetic due to strain-induced martensite transformation. Used in spring applications, structural members, and components requiring higher strength without heat treatment. Intermediate annealing required between severe forming operations.

Welding Characteristics

Fusion Welding: Excellent weldability using TIG (GTAW), MIG (GMAW), stick (SMAW), and automated processes (SAW, laser, electron beam). No preheating required. Filler metal matching or slightly overmatched to base: ER308/ER308L for SS304, ER316/ER316L for SS316. Low-carbon (L) grades prevent sensitization in as-welded condition. Argon or argon-helium shielding gas prevents oxidation and porosity. Back purging with argon maintains corrosion resistance on root side. Post-weld passivation recommended for optimal corrosion performance.

Resistance Welding: Spot, seam, and projection welding widely used in appliance and automotive manufacturing due to high electrical resistivity (720-900 nŒ©¬∑m) generating heat efficiently. Medium electrode force (2-4 kN) and welding current 10-20% higher than carbon steel. Electrode cooling critical as stainless conducts heat poorly. Machinability & Forming

Machining Considerations: Work hardening and low thermal conductivity make stainless more difficult to machine than carbon steel. Machinability rating 45% of B1112 free-machining steel. Sharp tools essential (CBN or PCD inserts for high-volume), reduced cutting speeds (50-60% of carbon steel), heavy feed rates, and sulfur-chlorine cutting fluids prevent work hardening ahead of tool. Free-machining grades 303/416 with sulfur additions improve machinability 100% for screw machine products.

Cold Forming: Minimum bend radius 1-1.5t for annealed material depending on thickness and bend axis relative to rolling direction (transverse bending more difficult). Springback 3-5¬∞ requires overbending. Drawing compounds and polished dies prevent galling. Roll forming, brake bending, stretching, and hydroforming commercially practiced. For severe draws exceeding 40% reduction, intermediate annealing necessary. Punch clearance 10-12% of thickness prevents edge cracking. Stainless steel drawing quality (DDQ) grades available for deep drawn cookware and sinks.

Chequered Plate Patterns & Configurations

Diamond Pattern (5-Bar Treadplate)

Pattern Geometry: Raised diamond-shaped bars arranged in parallel rows at 60¬∞ angles creating visually appealing and highly functional anti-slip surface. Bar height 0.5mm to 1.5mm above base plate depending on thickness (1:5 to 1:10 ratio pattern height to base thickness). Diamond pitch 25-40mm center-to-center providing optimal contact area and drainage channels. Pattern area coverage 35-45% raised, 55-65% flat.

Performance Characteristics: Coefficient of friction (COF) 0.60-0.75 dry, 0.45-0.60 wet conditions per ASTM F1679 exceeding building code minimums (0.50 dry, 0.42 wet). Excellent directional grip for foot traffic and wheeled vehicles. Self-cleaning design as dirt, debris, water drain through pattern valleys. Pattern remains effective even with 30-40% wear over 15-20 year service life. Most popular pattern accounting for 70% of chequered plate production worldwide.

Tear Drop / Pebble Pattern

Design Features: Rounded, teardrop-shaped raised protrusions arranged in staggered rows providing multi-directional traction. Protrusion height 1.0-2.0mm, base diameter 8-12mm, spacing 15-25mm. Smoother profile versus diamond pattern reduces noise from foot traffic and rolling loads while maintaining COF 0.55-0.70 dry, 0.40-0.55 wet. Less aggressive appearance preferred in commercial and public spaces.

Applications: Ideal for platforms, walkways, and access areas where foot comfort and noise reduction are priorities alongside slip resistance. Rounded profile easier on footwear, reducing wear on shoe soles. Facilitates easier cleaning with mechanical sweepers and pressure washing versus angular diamond pattern which can trap debris. Common in food processing facilities (stainless steel), chemical plants, and public infrastructure where ADA/accessibility compliance and frequent cleaning required.

Specialty Patterns - 3-Bar & Lentil

3-Bar Pattern: Three parallel raised bars running longitudinally spaced evenly across plate width. Bar width 3-6mm, height 1.0-1.5mm, spacing 20-35mm. Provides directional slip resistance ideal for ramps, inclined walkways, and vehicle access where primary traffic flow is predictable. Lower manufacturing cost than diamond or tear drop patterns (20-30% savings). Commonly used in dock levelers, loading ramps, and stairs where perpendicular traction is priority.

Lentil/Rice Grain Pattern: Small, elongated oval protrusions resembling lentils or rice grains, densely arranged in random or aligned arrays. Protrusion dimensions 6-10mm length, 3-5mm width, 0.8-1.2mm height. Highest pattern density (protrusions per cm¬≤) providing superior grip in compact areas. Used in stair treads, narrow platforms, and confined spaces where maximum traction needed. Aesthetic appeal for decorative applications in commercial interiors, elevator floors, and architectural features.

Pattern Manufacturing Process

Hot Rolling Method: Pattern rolled into steel at temperatures >1000¬∞C during final mill passes using engraved work rolls. Pattern depth 0.5-1.5mm, tolerances ¬±0.2mm. Hot-rolled chequered plate maintains uniform base thickness with pattern on one or both sides. Surface finish mill scale (black), pickled & oiled, or hot-dip galvanized. Most economical production method for carbon steel and low-alloy grades. Suitable for structural applications where dimensional precision less critical.

Cold Pressing/Embossing: Pattern pressed into cold sheet using hydraulic press with engraved male/female dies. Enables tighter tolerances (¬±0.1mm), finer patterns, and production on pre-finished materials (stainless steel, aluminum, pre-painted). Press capacity 300-2000 tons depending on plate size and pattern depth. Allows custom patterns and logos for branding applications. Higher cost (30-50% premium) offset by superior surface quality and dimensional accuracy. Used for stainless steel and aluminum chequered plates where appearance and corrosion resistance paramount.

Installation Methods & Maintenance

Mounting & Fastening

Welded Installation: Direct welding to structural steel supports using SMAW (stick), GMAW (MIG), or FCAW (flux-cored) processes. Intermittent welds 50-75mm length, 150-200mm spacing minimize distortion and allow thermal expansion. Weld on flat (underside) of plate preserving top pattern integrity. Grind weld spatter flush to prevent trip hazards. Touch-up welds with zinc-rich paint (galvanized) or matching paint system. Used in permanent industrial platforms, truck beds, stair treads welded to stringers.

Bolted/Screwed Installation: Mechanical fastening using countersunk screws, bolts, or blind rivets allows disassembly for access to underfloor utilities and replacement of worn sections. Pre-drill holes 1-2mm oversize for thermal expansion, use flat washers distributing load, and weatherproof sealant at penetrations. Fastener spacing 200-300mm perimeter, 400-600mm field depending on thickness and loading. Stainless steel fasteners prevent galvanic corrosion with aluminum. Ideal for removable access panels, equipment covers, modular platform systems.

Edge Treatment & Finishing

Edge Protection: Sheared edges sharp and prone to injury, requiring edge treatment: grinding/deburring smooth, folding/hemming 90¬∞ return, or welding angle iron nosing. Nosing provides finished appearance, stiffens panel edge, and protects pattern integrity at high-traffic boundaries. Yellow powder-coated nosing provides visual warning for drop-offs and platform edges per OSHA requirements. Threshold plates with beveled edges (1:12 slope max) ensure ADA wheelchair accessibility and reduce trip hazards.

Joint Detailing: Adjacent plates butt-jointed with 3-5mm gap for drainage and expansion, supported on continuous beams beneath. Lapped joints (50mm min overlap) for water-tight applications, sealed with butyl tape or polyurethane caulk. Expansion joints 10-15mm width every 15-20 meters accommodate thermal movement (12√ó10‚Åª‚Å∂/¬∞C for steel, 23√ó10‚Åª‚Å∂/¬∞C for aluminum). Cover plates or flexible joint fillers prevent debris accumulation and maintain safe walking surface.

Maintenance & Service Life

Routine Maintenance: Regular sweeping removes debris preventing pattern valley clogging which reduces slip resistance. Pressure washing (2000-3000 PSI) quarterly or semi-annually removes oil, grease, and embedded dirt. Degreasing detergents restore COF to near-new levels. For galvanized steel, inspect for coating breakdown (white rust, red rust) annually, touch-up with zinc-rich paint as needed. Stainless/aluminum require minimal maintenance beyond cleaning. Expected service life: MS painted 7-12 years indoor, MS galvanized 15-25 years outdoor, SS/aluminum 30-50 years with proper drainage.

Refurbishment: Worn patterns (>50% height loss) or corroded plates warrant replacement. Welded installations require cutting and re-welding. Bolted systems easily replaced by removing fasteners and installing new panels. Partial replacements possible by cutting out damaged sections and welding/bolting patches. Galvanized plates can be over-coated with epoxy systems extending life 5-10 years. Consider upgrading to stainless or aluminum in high-corrosion areas experiencing premature failure of carbon steel to reduce lifecycle costs.
