Valve Types & Configurations

Ball Valves

Design: Quarter-turn rotary design with spherical disc offering rapid shut-off capability. Available in floating ball configuration for sizes DN15 to DN200, ideal for moderate pressure applications, and trunnion mounted design for larger sizes DN50 to DN600, engineered for high-pressure systems up to 2500 PSI.

Flow Capacity: Flow coefficient (Cv) ranging from 2.0 for small process lines to 15,000 for large industrial pipelines. Full port design ensures minimal pressure drop across the valve, maintaining system efficiency and reducing energy costs.

Gate Valves

Construction: Multi-turn linear motion valves featuring wedge, knife, or parallel slide gate designs. Full bore construction provides unrestricted flow path with minimal turbulence. Available in sizes from DN15 to DN1200 for diverse industrial applications.

Stem Configuration: Rising stem (OS&Y - Outside Screw and Yoke) provides visual position indication, while non-rising stem design offers compact installation in space-constrained applications. Both configurations ensure reliable sealing and extended service life.

Butterfly Valves

Design Variants: Concentric (wafer/lug type) for general service, double offset (high-performance) for tighter shut-off and reduced operating torque, and triple offset (metal-seated) for extreme temperature and pressure applications up to 600¬∞C and 100 bar.

Size Range: Compact, lightweight construction available from DN50 to DN3000, making them ideal for high-flow, low-pressure drop applications. 90-degree quarter-turn operation enables rapid on/off control with reduced actuator sizing requirements.

Control Valves

Actuation: Pneumatic actuators (spring-return or double-acting) for fail-safe operation, electric actuators (MOV) for precise positioning with 0.1% resolution, and hydraulic actuators for extreme force requirements. All configurations available with digital positioners for enhanced control accuracy.

Rangeability: Exceptional 50:1 turndown ratio with characterized flow curves including equal percentage (logarithmic control), linear (proportional control), and quick opening (on/off service). Smart positioners provide real-time diagnostics and predictive maintenance capabilities.

Pressure Ranges: Vacuum -1 to 0 bar for process vessels, compound -1 to +15 bar for dual-phase systems, low pressure 0-60 PSI for air and pneumatic circuits, medium pressure 0-3000 PSI for hydraulic and process applications, high pressure 0-15,000 PSI for extreme service including hydraulic power units and injection systems.

Digital Pressure Gauges

Display Technology: High-contrast LCD displays with 5-digit resolution providing 0.01% readout precision, backlit LED displays for low-light environments, dual-line displays showing pressure and temperature simultaneously, and large 25mm character height for visibility up to 10 meters. Configurable engineering units (bar, PSI, kPa, MPa, kg/cm¬≤).

Smart Features: Min/max/average recording with timestamp, programmable dual setpoint alarms with relay outputs, data logging capability storing up to 10,000 readings, USB and Bluetooth connectivity for remote monitoring and configuration. Battery life up to 3 years on single lithium cell with auto-shutoff feature.

Precision Test Gauges

Accuracy Standards: EN 837-1 Class 0.1 providing ¬±0.1% full-scale accuracy for calibration laboratories, Class 0.6 (¬±0.6%) for field reference standards, and Class 1.0-2.5 for general industrial use. ASME B40.100 Grade 4A (¬±0.1% span) and Grade A (¬±0.5% span) master test gauges with mirror scale and knife-edge pointer for parallax-free reading.

Construction & Materials

Case Materials: Painted steel for cost-effective general purpose applications, SS304 polished stainless for hygienic food/pharma use, SS316L for marine and corrosive environments, phenolic and polycarbonate for electrical isolation and lightweight applications. Flow Meter Technologies & Configurations

Electromagnetic Flow Meters (Magflow)

Working Principle: Based on Faraday‚Äôs Law of Electromagnetic Induction, suitable for conductive liquids such as water, wastewater, slurries, chemicals, and food-grade liquids.

Accuracy: ¬±0.2% of reading, ideal for billing, batching, and custody-transfer applications.

Lining Options: PTFE, PFA, Neoprene, and Rubber to withstand corrosive and abrasive media.

Ultrasonic Flow Meters

Design Variants: Transit-time, Doppler, and Clamp-on for non-invasive measurement on metal or PVC pipes.

Applications: HVAC monitoring, chilled water, large pipelines, non-contact measurement, and harsh or contaminated fluids.

Benefits: Zero pressure drop, leak-proof, and ideal for temporary survey installations.

Turbine Flow Meters

Construction: Precision-machined rotor with stainless steel bearings delivering high repeatability and linearity.

Flow Range: Suitable for low-viscosity liquids such as fuels, solvents, and chemicals.

Features: Fast response time, high accuracy (¬±0.5%), and customizable pulse output for PLC/SCADA integration.

Rotameters (Variable Area Flow Meters)

Working: Float rises with flow rate, providing direct visual indication on a calibrated scale. Available in glass, acrylic, and metal tube designs.

Advantages: Cost-effective, no power supply required, ideal for industrial gas and liquid distribution lines.
