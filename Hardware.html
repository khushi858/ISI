Fastener Types & Configurations

Hexagon Head Bolts

Design: Full-thread and partial-thread configurations with precision-machined hexagon heads conforming to DIN 931, DIN 933, ISO 4014, and ISO 4017 standards. Available in metric sizes M6 to M48 with coarse (standard) and fine thread pitch options for specialized applications.

Thread Types: Metric ISO threads for European/Asian markets, UNC (Unified Coarse) and UNF (Unified Fine) threads for North American applications. Thread tolerance classes 6g for standard fit, 6h for close fit assemblies. Thread length calculated per DIN 960 for optimal clamping force distribution.

Socket Head Cap Screws (SHCS)

Construction: High-strength internal hexagon drive (Allen key) design per DIN 912 and ISO 4762, providing superior torque transmission and compact head profile. Ideal for counterbored holes and applications requiring flush mounting or aesthetic appearance.

Strength Grades: Class 12.9 (1220 MPa tensile strength) for high-stress structural connections, Class 10.9 (1040 MPa) for general engineering applications, Class 8.8 (800 MPa) for standard industrial use. Socket sizes from 2.5mm to 36mm accommodate various torque requirements.

Stud Bolts & Double-End Studs

Design Variants: Continuous thread (full-length threaded) for adjustable clamping, double-end studs with 1D, 1.5D, or 2D thread engagement lengths for permanent installation in tapped holes. Tap-end studs feature reduced shank diameter for thermal expansion compensation in flange connections.

Applications: ASME B16.5 and API 6A flange connections in pressure vessels, heat exchangers, and piping systems. Available with rolled or cut threads, heavy hex nuts, and hardened washers as complete stud bolt sets rated for pressures up to 15,000 PSI and temperatures to 650¬∞C.

Anchor Bolts & Foundation Fasteners

Types: L-bolt (bent anchor) for light structural loads, J-bolt with 90¬∞ hook for moderate pullout resistance, headed anchor bolts with forged heads for heavy equipment foundations. Available in galvanized carbon steel, stainless steel 304/316, and high-strength alloy steel grades.

Specifications: Sizes from M12 to M64 (1/2" to 2.5") with embedment lengths from 150mm to 1500mm. Design load capacity per ACI 318 and AISC standards. Chemical anchor systems with epoxy or polyester resin for post-installation in concrete, masonry, and stone with seismic and fire ratings.

Nuts & Washers - Types & Specifications

Hexagon Nuts - Standard & Specialty

Nut Types: Full hex nuts DIN 934/ISO 4032 for standard applications, heavy hex nuts ASME B18.2.2 for structural bolting with increased bearing area, jam nuts (half-height) for lock-nut applications, slotted castle nuts with cotter pin holes for safety-critical assemblies, and nylon-insert lock nuts with vibration resistance.

Grades & Coatings: Property Class 8 (800 MPa proof load) matching Grade 8.8 bolts, Class 10 for Grade 10.9/12.9 high-strength bolts. Surface treatments include zinc electroplating (5-15 microns), hot-dip galvanizing (50-85 microns), Dacromet coating for superior corrosion protection, and PTFE dry-film lubricant for controlled torque assembly.

Washers - Flat, Spring & Specialty

Flat Washers: Form A (normal series) per DIN 125/ISO 7089 for general load distribution, Form B (large series) with increased outer diameter for soft materials and oversize holes, Form C (extra large) for timber construction and oversized clearances. Fender washers with 3:1 OD:ID ratio for maximum bearing area.

Spring Washers: Helical spring washers (split lock) DIN 127 providing axial tension and anti-loosening action, wave washers for controlled compression in assemblies, Belleville (conical disc) washers for high-load applications with predictable deflection characteristics. Tooth lock washers with internal/external serrations bite into mating surfaces.

Titanium Grade 2/5 for aerospace, marine, and chemical processing with exceptional strength-to-weight ratio.

Threaded Rod Types & Configurations

Fully Threaded Rods (All-Thread)

Design: Continuous thread along entire length from M6 to M48 diameter, available in standard lengths of 1m, 2m, and 3m. Custom lengths up to 6m available for special applications. Thread form per ISO 68-1 metric coarse pitch or DIN 976-1 for European markets, ASME B18.31.3 for unified inch threads.

Thread Quality: Rolled threads (Class 8.8 and higher) provide 10-15% greater tensile strength than cut threads through cold-work hardening and uninterrupted grain flow. Cut threads available for larger diameters (M56-M100) and exotic materials. Thread tolerance 6g for free-running assembly with standard nuts, 6h for tighter fit in precision applications.

Partially Threaded Rods (Half-Thread)

Construction: Smooth shank section with threaded ends, typically 2D to 3D thread length (D = nominal diameter) on each end. Smooth section reduces stress concentration and allows thermal expansion in high-temperature flange connections. Thread transition features rolled or undercut relief to minimize fatigue crack initiation.

Applications: Ideal for ASME B16.5 flange bolting where threads should not engage in the pressure boundary, suspended piping systems requiring vertical adjustment, and structural connections with long grip lengths. The smooth shank section provides superior fatigue performance compared to fully threaded rods under cyclic loading.

Threaded Rod Couplers & Connectors

Coupling Nuts: Hex coupling nuts (also called extension nuts) per DIN 6334 with length equal to 3 times the diameter, enabling rigid joining of threaded rods for extended reach applications. Right-hand or left-hand threads available for turnbuckle-style tensioning assemblies. Heavy hex pattern provides adequate wrench engagement.

Turnbuckles: Adjustable tensioning devices with opposing right-hand and left-hand threads allowing length adjustment without rotation of connected members. Hook, eye, and jaw end fittings accommodate wire rope, chain, and structural connection requirements. Forged carbon steel or stainless steel construction with hot-dip galvanized or electropolished finishes.

Anchor Bolt Systems

Cast-In-Place Anchors: L-bolts and J-bolts with 90¬∞ or 180¬∞ hooks embedded in fresh concrete during pour, providing mechanical interlock. Headed anchor bolts with forged heads per ASTM F1554 Grade 36, 55, or 105 for structural steel base plate connections. Embedment depth calculated per ACI 318 Chapter 17 for tension and shear capacity.

Post-Installed Anchors: Mechanical expansion anchors (wedge, sleeve, undercut) for installation in hardened concrete with ICC-ES ESR approval. Chemical adhesive anchors using epoxy (high-load), polyester (fast-cure), or vinyl ester (moisture-tolerant) systems per ASTM E488. Seismic and fire ratings available for life-safety applications.

U-Bolt Types & Configurations

Round Bend U-Bolts (Pipe Clamps)

Design: Semi-circular bend matching pipe outer diameter with two threaded legs passing through saddle or mounting plate. Inside diameter (ID) sized to pipe nominal bore: DN15-DN600 (1/2" to 24" NPS). Bend radius precision-formed to maintain uniform clamping pressure around full pipe circumference, preventing localized stress concentration.

Thread Configuration: Coarse metric threads M6 to M48 or unified coarse UNC 1/4" to 2" for maximum clamping force with standard hex nuts. Thread length typically 2.5D to 3D (D = nominal diameter) to accommodate double-nut lockdown, spring washers, and saddle thickness. Right-angle thread ends machined perpendicular to leg axis for proper washer seating.

Square Bend U-Bolts (Structural Mounting)

Construction: Square or rectangular bend geometry designed to capture I-beams, channel iron (C-channel), angle iron, and square/rectangular hollow sections (RHS/SHS). Inside dimensions correspond to standard structural steel sections per AISC, BS, or DIN standards. Sharp 90¬∞ corners or radiused bends depending on load distribution requirements.

Load Distribution: Wide mounting plates (often 6mm-12mm thick) distribute clamping forces across greater surface area, reducing bearing stress on thin-walled structural members. Ribbed or gusseted plates provide bending rigidity preventing plate deflection under high clamping torque. Suitable for beam hanger assemblies supporting HVAC ductwork, cable trays, and conveyor systems.

Semi-Round & Custom Bend U-Bolts

Semi-Round Design: Flattened top section with round bottom bend, combining advantages of round pipe clamping with flat top surface for direct mounting to beam flanges or flat plates. Particularly useful for securing conduit, cable bundles, and small-diameter pipe to structural steel without separate saddles or backing plates.

Custom Configurations: J-bolts (single leg anchor), L-bolts (90¬∞ hook), eye bolts (forged eye for rigging), and specialty shapes for unique mounting geometries. CNC tube bending enables complex multi-axis bends for automotive exhaust hangers, agricultural implements, and custom machinery fixtures. Prototype and short-run production available.

Heavy-Duty Industrial U-Bolts

Construction: Forged rather than bent construction for extreme-load applications where bend radius fatigue is concern. Material upsizing (e.g., 16mm diameter rod for M12 thread) provides greater bending stiffness and fatigue resistance. Tangent legs (straight sections) extended to 100-200mm (4"-8") accommodate thick insulation, multiple pipes in rack, or offset mounting requirements.

Special Features: Thread rolled after bending preserves material grain flow and tensile strength (no HAZ from welding). Thread protectors (plastic caps) prevent damage during shipping and storage. Identification marking (size, grade, heat number) via roll-stamping or laser etching for traceability in critical applications like nuclear power and aerospace.

Pipe Clamps, Brackets & Support Systems

Cushioned Pipe Clamps (Vibration Isolation)

Design: Steel U-bolt with bonded rubber or elastomer liner preventing metal-to-metal contact. Rubber compound durometer (hardness) selected for application: soft (40-50 Shore A) for maximum vibration damping in HVAC, medium (60-70 Shore A) for general water pipe, hard (80-90 Shore A) for high-temperature steam to 100¬∞C.

Benefits: Eliminates galvanic corrosion between dissimilar metals (steel clamp on copper pipe), reduces noise transmission by 15-25 dB in building services, prevents abrasive wear on FRP and PVC pipe, accommodates minor thermal expansion/contraction without stress concentration. UV-resistant EPDM rubber for outdoor exposure, Nitrile for oil/fuel lines, Neoprene for general purpose.

Two-Piece Split Clamps (Hinged Design)

Construction: Bolted two-piece clamshell design allowing installation on existing pipe runs without disconnection. Hinged models with spring-loaded hinge pin enable one-handed installation. Precision-machined semi-circular grooves provide 360¬∞ pipe contact with uniform pressure distribution. Available with or without rubber/plastic liners.

Variants: Riser clamps for vertical pipe support with integral load-bearing collar, guide/slide clamps with low-friction PTFE washers permitting axial thermal movement while restricting lateral displacement, and sprinkler head clamps for fire protection systems with drop-rod threaded holes at precise angles per NFPA 13 requirements.

Beam Clamps & Structural Attachments

Types: Flange clamps with serrated jaws gripping I-beam top flange, providing vertical hanging points for trapeze hangers and threaded rod suspensions. Adjustable jaw opening accommodates flange thickness variations 6-25mm. Rated load capacities 100kg to 2000kg depending on size and material grade.

Specialized Designs: Girder clamps with side-mount threaded holes for horizontal pipe attachment. Purlin clamps with C-section profile matching corrugated roof purlins in industrial buildings. Trolley beam clamps with rolling wheels for movable lifting points on overhead monorail systems. Drop forged construction provides superior strength-to-weight ratio versus fabricated steel.
