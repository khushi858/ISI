Angle Types & Configurations

Equal Leg Angles (L-Sections)

Design: L-shaped structural profiles with legs of equal length forming 90¬∞ angle per IS 808 (Indian Standard Medium Angles) and BS EN 10056-1 specifications. Available in sizes from 25√ó25mm to 200√ó200mm with leg thickness ranging from 3mm to 25mm. Dimensional Standards: Leg width tolerance ¬±1.5mm for sizes up to 100mm, ¬±2mm for larger sections per IS 1852. Root radius typically 3.5-7mm depending on size, providing smooth stress transition at the junction. Squareness of legs maintained within 1¬∞ tolerance ensuring proper fit-up in bolted and welded connections. Surface finish as-rolled with mill scale for standard applications or shot-blasted for painted finishes.

Unequal Leg Angles

Construction: Asymmetric L-sections with different leg lengths optimized for directional loading conditions per IS 808 Part 2. Common sizes include 40√ó25mm, 50√ó30mm, 65√ó45mm, 75√ó50mm, 100√ó65mm, 125√ó75mm, 150√ó100mm, and 200√ó100mm. Structural Properties: Centroidal axis location offset from geometric center requiring careful consideration in design calculations. Connection Methods & Joining Techniques

Bolted Connections

Fastener Selection: High-strength structural bolts Grade 8.8 or 10.9 per IS 1367 provide superior clamping force and shear/tensile capacity. Bolt diameters M12, M16, M20, M24 most common with holes drilled or punched 2mm oversized allowing assembly tolerance. Minimum edge distance 1.5d and spacing 2.5d (d=bolt diameter) prevents splitting and ensures load distribution per IS 800 design code.

Connection Types: Simple shear connections with single or double angles as web cleats transfer vertical shear loads while allowing member end rotation. Moment connections with bolted end plates provide rotational restraint for rigid frame action. Bracket connections using angles with stiffeners support concentrated loads from beams or equipment. Friction-grip bolts (HSFG) develop clamping force preventing slip under service loads in seismic and fatigue-critical applications.

Welded Connections

Weld Types: Fillet welds along angle legs most economical joining method with throat thickness calculated per IS 816 to develop required strength. Full penetration groove welds for moment-resisting connections provide 100% base metal strength. Slot and plug welds connect overlapping angle legs in built-up sections. Minimum weld size 3mm for leg thickness <10mm, 5mm for 10-20mm thickness preventing rapid cooling and cracking.

Welding Standards: AWS D1.1 Structural Welding Code specifies prequalified joint geometries, filler metal requirements (E7018 electrodes or ER70S-6 wire), and inspection criteria. Welding procedure specifications (WPS) document voltage, amperage, travel speed, and interpass temperature controlling heat input and preventing distortion. Visual inspection identifies surface discontinuities while ultrasonic testing (UT) reveals internal defects in critical connections. Certified welders per AWS QC-1 ensure consistent quality.

ISLC - Indian Standard Light Channels

PFC & UPN European Channels

PFC (Parallel Flange Channel): European equivalent to ISMC with parallel inner and outer flange faces per BS 4-1 and EN 10279 standards. UPN (U-Profile Normal): European C-channel per DIN 1026-1 and EN 10365 with tapered flanges similar to ISMC geometry. Common in Continental European markets. Sizes range 80mm to 400mm depth. Interchangeability with ISMC limited due to subtle dimensional differences in flange width, thickness, and root radius. Selection depends on project specification requirements, supplier availability, and connection detail compatibility.

Thermo-Mechanical Treatment Process

TMT Manufacturing Technology

Three-Stage Process: Stage 1 - Hot rolling at 1000-1100¬∞C through continuous mill train produces ribbed bar to final diameter. Stage 2 - Rapid water quenching (Tempcore process) immediately after final stand cools outer surface to 100-150¬∞C forming hardened martensitic rim while core remains austenitic at 800-900¬∞C. Stage 3 - Self-tempering as core heat conducts outward transforms martensite to tempered martensite and core transforms to ferrite-pearlite creating optimal microstructure gradient. Total process time 45-90 seconds from rolling to cooling bed.

Microstructural Gradient: Outer layer (thickness 1-3mm) contains tempered martensite with 500-600 HV hardness providing high strength and wear resistance. Intermediate zone with fine bainitic structure transitions strength gradually preventing brittle interface. Core region contains ductile ferrite-pearlite with 150-200 HV hardness maintaining elongation and bend properties. This metallurgical gradient combines strength of hardened steel with ductility of mild steel in single bar impossible through conventional heat treatment.
